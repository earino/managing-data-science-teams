## Rating Recommendation

| Rating | Definition |
|--------|-----------|
| **Exceeds Expectations** | Consistently delivers impact beyond what is expected at this level; operates at the scope of the next level |
| **Meets Expectations** | Delivers what is expected at this level; reliable, competent, and growing |
| **Below Expectations** | Not consistently meeting the expectations for this level; requires a development plan |

**Your Recommendation:**

**Justification (3-5 sentences):** Tie your rating explicitly to level expectations. Reference specific accomplishments and development areas. Explain WHY this rating, not just WHAT the rating is. A calibration reviewer should be able to read this justification and understand your reasoning without having met the person.

**Example justification:**

> "Meets Expectations for L4. Jordan delivered solid, reliable analysis throughout the review period, including the churn model and the pricing analysis. Both were well-received by stakeholders and delivered on time. However, both projects were scoped by the manager rather than independently identified by Jordan, which is the key differentiator between Meets and Exceeds at L4. For an Exceeds rating, I would need to see Jordan proactively identifying high-impact problems and scoping the work independently."

## Evaluation Criteria

Your performance summary will be assessed on the following dimensions:

| Criterion | Excellent | Satisfactory | Needs Revision |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| **Impact Quantification** | Every accomplishment includes specific, quantified business impact (revenue, cost savings, adoption rates, etc.) | Most accomplishments are quantified; one or two lack specific numbers | Accomplishments describe activities ("built a dashboard") without explaining business impact |
| **Level Calibration** | Rating justification explicitly references level expectations and explains why this person is at/above/below | Some reference to level expectations | Rating is asserted without connecting to what's expected at this level |
| **Specificity** | Development areas are concrete and include actionable recommendations | Development areas are identifiable but somewhat vague | Development areas are generic ("improve communication") |
| **Evidence Quality** | Supporting evidence includes dated, specific manager log entries and stakeholder feedback | Some evidence provided but lacks dates or specificity | No supporting evidence; rating is based on general impressions |
| **Calibration Readiness** | Summary could be presented in a calibration session and withstand peer scrutiny | Summary is mostly complete but may need additional evidence to survive calibration | Summary would not hold up to peer challenge in a calibration session |
| **Case Context Fit** | Role context, accomplishments, and expectations are clearly tied to the specific case context | Some connection to case context | Summary is generic and could apply to any organization |

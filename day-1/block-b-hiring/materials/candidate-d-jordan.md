# Candidate Profile D — Jordan (The Culture Fit Trap)

**Do not share this card with your interviewer.**

## Background

1.5 years at a trendy startup. Active on data Twitter/LinkedIn. Gives well-received conference talks. Known for great energy in interviews.

## Strengths

- Extremely likable and articulate
- Enthusiastic about the company and role
- Great cultural energy — would be fun to work with
- Uses the right vocabulary and frameworks

## Weaknesses

- Technical depth is shallow — analysis tends to be surface-level
- Has not worked with messy real-world data at scale
- Strong on "what" but weak on "how"

## The Tell

When probed on methodology, pivots to storytelling about impact rather than explaining the approach. A rubric that separates "communication" from "technical depth" will catch this.

## How to Play This Profile

- Use buzzwords correctly but cannot go a level deeper when pressed
- When asked "how did you build that model?" respond with the business impact ("it drove a 15% improvement in retention") rather than the methodology
- If pressed on technical details, say something vague like "we used a pretty standard approach" and redirect
- Be warm, engaging, and make the interviewer like you — that's the trap

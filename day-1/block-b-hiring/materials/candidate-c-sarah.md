# Candidate Profile C — Sarah (The Wrong-Level Candidate)

**Do not share this card with your interviewer.**

## Background

8 years in analytics. Led a team of 5 at a large enterprise. PhD in statistics. Applying because she wants a "fresh start" at a smaller company.

## Strengths

- Deep technical expertise and impressive resume
- Articulate about organizational challenges and team dynamics
- Can discuss advanced methodology confidently

## Weaknesses

- Too senior for a mid-level IC role — will be bored within 3 months
- Expects to influence strategy from day one
- May not be willing to do the hands-on execution the role requires

## The Tell

Keeps steering conversation toward team building and strategy rather than hands-on execution. A good interviewer will notice the role mismatch.

## How to Play This Profile

- Answer questions about execution with "I would delegate that" or "I would design the framework for that"
- Mention your team management experience frequently
- When asked about a specific analysis, describe the approach at a high level but redirect to how you'd set up the process
- Express genuine interest in the company but subtly signal you see this as a stepping stone to management

**Quick reference:**
- **R (Responsible):** Does the work. Multiple Rs are okay.
- **A (Accountable):** Owns the outcome and has final say. Exactly ONE per decision.
- **C (Consulted):** Provides input BEFORE the decision. Keep this list short.
- **I (Informed):** Notified AFTER the decision. Default for most people.

## Escalation Path

*When RACI breaks down — when people disagree about who is A, or when the A makes a decision that a C strongly objects to — what happens? Define the escalation path for your team.*

### When to Escalate
- The "A" and a "C" fundamentally disagree after discussion
- A decision is blocked because no one claims accountability
- A Type 1 (irreversible) decision needs higher authority
- A stakeholder outside the RACI tries to override the decision

### Escalation Levels

| Level | Who | When | How |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Level 1** | *(e.g., Analytics Manager)* | *(First attempt to resolve within the team)* | *(Sync meeting or decision memo)* |
| **Level 2** | *(e.g., VP / Sponsor)* | *(Team cannot resolve; cross-functional disagreement)* | *(Brief written summary + 15-min meeting)* |
| **Level 3** | *(e.g., C-suite / Exec team)* | *(Strategic disagreement with org-wide implications)* | *(Decision memo to exec sponsor)* |

### Escalation Principles
- **Escalate with options, not problems.** Always bring at least two options and your recommendation.
- **Escalate promptly.** Sitting on a blocked decision for two weeks is worse than escalating on day two.
- **Escalate in writing.** Verbal escalations get lost. A one-paragraph summary ensures everyone has the same context.
- **No skipping levels.** Level 1 must be attempted before Level 2.

## Evaluation Criteria

Your RACI Matrix will be assessed on the following dimensions:

| Criterion | Weight | What "Good" Looks Like |
|---|---|---|
| **Appropriate decision selection** | 25% | The 5-8 decisions chosen are genuinely important and commonly confusing for analytics teams in your case context. Not trivial decisions. |
| **Correct RACI assignments** | 25% | Exactly one A per decision. Rs are people who actually do the work. Cs are kept to a minimum. Assignments reflect realistic organizational dynamics. |
| **Context sensitivity** | 20% | RACI assignments differ meaningfully between Small/Medium/Large contexts. A startup where the manager does everything looks different from an enterprise with specialized roles. |
| **Escalation path clarity** | 15% | Clear, practical escalation levels with specific triggers. Principles are actionable. |
| **Completeness** | 15% | All sections filled in. At least 5 decisions fully RACI'd. Escalation path defined. |
